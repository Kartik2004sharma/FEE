Book Name: Harry Potter

Author: J. K. Rowling

Harry Potter is a series of fantasy novels written by British author J. K. Rowling. The novels chronicle the lives of a young wizard, Harry Potter, and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, all of whom are students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The main story arc concerns Harry's quest to overcome the Dark wizard Lord Voldemort, who aims to become immortal, enslave humanity, and destroy all non-magical beings. The final novel, Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows, was released on 21 July 200 7, and sold 11 million copies in the first 24 hours of its release. The series has also had a massive impact on popular culture and was listed in a 2007 BBC poll on the UK's Best Loved Books. At the time of the last publication in 2007, the series, collectively known as the Harry Potter book series, had sold over 400 million copies, been translated into 67 languages, and the last four books have consecutively set records as the fastest-selling books in history.

Book Name: Crime and Punishment

Author: Fyodor Dostoevsky

"Crime and Punishment" by Fyodor Dostoevsky is a psychological drama set in St. Petersburg, Russia.
The novel follows Rodion Raskolnikov, a destitute former student who rationalizes that he can transcend morality to perform a "necessary" murder. He kills a pawnbroker, Alyona Ivanovna, but is haunted by guilt and paranoia. His mental turmoil deepens as he interacts with various characters, including his family, the virtuous prostitute Sonia, and the astute detective Porfiry. Sonia represents redemption, urging Raskolnikov towards confession and spiritual awakening. The relentless Porfiry subtly pressures Raskolnikov, leading to his eventual confession. The novel explores themes of morality, justice, and redemption, examining the psychological impact of crime. Raskolnikov’s journey from hubris to humility underscores Dostoevsky’s exploration of the human condition, ultimately suggesting that true punishment and salvation are internal processes driven by one's conscience and faith.

Book Name: 12 Rules for Life

Author: Jordan B Peterson

"12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos" by Jordan B Peterson is a self-help book that offers practical advice for living a meaningful and fulfilling life. The book is divided into 12 rules, each of which addresses a specific aspect of life and provides guidance on how to navigate it. The rules cover topics such as setting boundaries, taking responsibility for one's actions, and finding meaning in life. Peterson draws on a wide range of sources, including psychology, philosophy, and religion, to support his arguments and provide readers with a comprehensive framework for understanding and improving their lives. The book has been widely praised for its practicality and accessibility, and has been translated into multiple languages. He discusses the necessity of embracing life's difficulties and the benefits of maintaining a strong moral compass. Each rule is enriched with insights from various disciplines, personal anecdotes, and cultural references, aiming to help individuals create order from chaos and find purpose amidst life's inherent struggles.
